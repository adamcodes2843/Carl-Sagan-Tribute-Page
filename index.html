Carl Sagan

A dedicated and inspirational advocate for science. One in billions and billions.

"Somewhere, Something Incredible is Waiting to be Known"

“It Has Been Said That Astronomy is a Humbling and Character-Building Experience.”

Scientific Achievements

  • Pioneered the field of Exobiology
  • Twice awarded NASA's Distguished Public Service Medal for extraordinary contributions during service
  • Briefed astronauts during the Apollo missions to the Moon
  • Experimenter on the Mariner, Viking, Voyager, and Galileo expeditions
  • Demonstrated amino acid formation from basic chemicals and radiation
  • Determined the high temperatures on Venus were due to the greenhouse effect, confirmed by Mariner 2
  • Hypothesized Saturn's moon Titan had liquid on the surface and that its reddish haze was due to raining of organic compounds
  • Hypothesized Jupiter's moon Europa had liquid water below its surface, potentially making it habitable
  • Chairman of the Division of Planetary Sciences of the American Astronomical Society
  • President of the Planetology Section of the American Geophysical Union
  • Chairman of the Astronomy Section of the American Association for the Advancement of Science
  • President and cofounder of the Planetary Society
  • Editor in Chief of Icarus, the most popular professional planetary research journal
  • Professor of Astronomy and director of the Laboratory of Planetary Studies at Cornell University for nearly 30 years until is his death in 1996

Carl Sagan posing with a model of the Viking lander in Death Valley, California.

Popularizing Science

  • Co-wrote and starred in Cosmos: A Personal Voyage
    • Seen by 500 million people in 60 countries
    • Two Emmy awards, a Hugo and a Peabody
  • Authored many best selling books:
    • Cosmos became the highest selling science book in the English language
    • The Dragons of Eden: Speculations on the Evolution of Human Intelligence won a Pulitzer Prize for General Non-Fiction
    • Contact, a Hugo winning best selling fiction novel, was the basis of the 1997 film with the same name
    • A Pale Blue Dot: A vision of the Human Future in Space was released as a sequel to Cosmos
    • The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Darkness was released months before his death in 1996 and a collections of novels titled Billions and Billions was released the following year.
  • Awarded the Public Welfare Medal from the National Acadamy of Sciences "For his ability to communicate the wonder and importance of science, to capture the imagination of so many, and to explain difficult concepts of science in understandable terms."
  • Received 20 honorary degrees from colleges and universities for his contributions to science, literature, education, and the preservation of the environment.

The Cosmic Calendar, popularized on Cosmos, scales the age of the universe, 13.7 billion years, down to 12 months.

"Science is More Than a Body of Knowledge; it is a Way of Thinking"


Science is more than a body of knowledge; it is a way of thinking. I have a foreboding of an America in my children's or grandchildren's time – when the United States is a service and information economy; when nearly all the key manufacturing industries have slipped away to other countries; when awesome technological powers are in the hands of a very few, and no one representing the public interest can even grasp the issues; when the people have lost the ability to set their own agendas or knowledgeably question those in authority; when, clutching our crystals and nervously consulting our horoscopes, our critical faculties in decline, unable to distinguish between what feels good and what's true, we slide, almost without noticing, back into superstition and darkness...The dumbing down of America is most evident in the slow decay of substantive content in the enormously influential media, the 30 second sound bites (now down to 10 seconds or less), lowest common denominator programming, credulous presentations on pseudoscience and superstition, but especially a kind of celebration of ignorance.
